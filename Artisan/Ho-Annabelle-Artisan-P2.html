BTS (Bangtan Boys)

BTS is a seven-member Korean boy band formed by Big Hit Entertainment. They are also known as Bangtan Boys or Bangtan Sonyeondan, which means Bulletproof Boy Scouts. They sing several genres of music including Korean pop, hip-hop, and R&B. They debuted in 2013 with the song No More Dream.

All the BTS members were born in Korea, and their songs are also all in Korean. BTS is often praised for being one of the most popular boy groups in South Korea. Unlike other top bands, they built their own fame with the small entertainment company Big Hit, while their competetiors top were all formed through large conglomerate entertainment companies. BTS is unique because their members often participate in composing their own songs. The boys of BTS finally had their first win on a weekly music charts broadcasting show three years after their debut in 2015 for their song "I Need U".

Aside from being nationally popular, BTS is famous internationally too. BTS is the first and only Korean act to have the same album spend more than one week as No. 1 on Billboard's World Albums Charts. BTS has spent more time in first place on the Billboard World Album Chart than any other K-pop group yet.
